### üìö Lesson 1.2: The Matrix (How It Actually Works)
*   **Objective:** Explain the relationship between You, Lenders, Bureaus, and FICO without using jargon.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   The credit system is a data game with three main players. You need to know who is who to win.
    *   **1. The Data Furnishers (The Teachers):** These are your banks, credit card companies, and lenders. They report your behavior (grades) to the bureaus.
    *   **2. The Data Collectors (The Gossips):** Equifax, TransUnion, and Experian. They don't make decisions; they just write down everything the Teachers say‚Äîgood or bad, true or false.
    *   **3. The Scorekeeper (The Admissions Officer):** FICO (and VantageScore). They look at the messy notes from the Gossips and calculate a single number (300-850) to predict risk.

*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "Data Triangle"** ‚Äî You vs. The Data Furnishers vs. The Data Collectors. Know who to fight.
*   **Moo Point Action:** Log in to Credit Lab and identify which of the "Three Gossips" (Bureaus) has the lowest score for you. Why is it lower?

### üìö Lesson 1.4: Debt vs. Leverage
*   **Objective:** Distinguish between "Toxic Debt" and "Power Leverage" to build a wealthy mindset.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Not all debt is created equal.
    *   **Toxic Debt (Liabilities):** Buying things that lose value or disappear. Shoes, vacations, dinners, cars that just drive you to work. You pay interest to look rich.
    *   **Power Leverage (Assets):** Buying things that PAY YOU. Real estate, business inventory, education that increases income. You pay interest to GET rich.
    *   **The Golden Rule:** Credit U scholars pay their "Toxic" credit card debt in full every month. We never pay interest on a pair of sneakers. We only pay interest on assets that earn more than the interest costs.
*   **Real-World Example:**
    *   *Toxic:* Borrowing $1,000 at 20% interest for a TV. You lose money every month.
    *   *Leverage:* Borrowing $1,000 at 10% interest to buy a lawnmower for a business that earns $2,000. You make profit using OPM (Other People's Money).
*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "ROI Filter"** ‚Äî Before swiping your card, ask: "Will this purchase put money back in my pocket?" If no, pay cash or don't buy it.
*   **Moo Point Action:** detailed review of your last statement. Circle one "Toxic" purchase you could have avoided.

### üìö Lesson 1.5: The Rules of Engagement (FCRA Rights)
*   **Objective:** Introduce the concept that the credit report is a legal document, and it must be 100% accurate.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) is your "Bill of Rights." It gives you the power to fight the giants.
    *   **Rule #1: Accuracy.** Everything on your report must be 100% accurate. If the date, balance, or name is even slightly wrong, it must go.
    *   **Rule #2: Verification.** If you challenge an item, the bureau MUST prove it's yours with evidence. If they can't find the contract, they must delete it.
    *   **Rule #3: Timeliness.** They have 30 days to answer your dispute. If they are silent, they lose.
    *   You are not asking for a favor; you are demanding federal compliance.
*   **Real-World Example:**
    *   If a police officer writes a speeding ticket but puts the wrong license plate number, the ticket is thrown out in court. It doesn't matter if you were speeding; the *record* is flawed. The same applies to credit.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **"Guilty Until Proven Innocent" (Flipped)** ‚Äî In Credit Law, the CREDITOR is on trial. They must prove the debt is valid. If they can't, you win.
*   **Moo Point Action:** Memorize the phrase: "Maximum Possible Accuracy." This is your sword.
*   **Knowledge Check:**
    1.  What law protects your credit report? (FCRA)
    2.  How many days do bureaus usually have to respond? (30)

### üìö Lesson 2.1: Reading the Scoreboard
*   **Objective:** Decode the confusing layout of a credit report to find errors.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Credit reports are intentionally boring and confusing. We decode them.
    *   **Section 1: Personal Info.** (Names, Addresses). Watch for errors here‚Äîwrong addresses can link you to other people's bad debt!
    *   **Section 2: Trade Lines.** (Your Accounts). This is the meat. Look for "Status" (Current/Late) and "Balance."
    *   **Section 3: Inquiries.** (Who looked). Hard vs. Soft.
    *   **Section 4: Public Records/Collections.** The "Red Flags."
*   **Credit U Framework:** **"Anatomy of the File"** ‚Äî Treat it like a medical chart. If there's a virus (Collection), we need to isolate it.
*   **Moo Point:** Go through your report. Highlight every single name or address that is old or misspelled. We will delete those later.

### üìö Lesson 2.2: The 35% Rule (Perfect Payment)
*   **Objective:** Strategies to ensure you never miss a payment again.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Payment History is 35% of your score. You cannot afford a "zero" in this category.
    *   **The Trap:** Relying on memory. Life gets busy. You *will* forget.
    *   **The Strategy:** Automation is the ultimate shield.
    *   **The Method:** Set up **Autopay for the Minimum Due** on every single account.
    *   *Wait, minimum?* Yes. You should still pay the full balance manually to save interest. But if you get sick/busy/lost, the Autopay catches you. It prevents the "Late Payment" mark.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "Autopilot Shield"** ‚Äî Build a safety net so you never accidentally fall.
*   **Moo Point:** Log into your bank *right now* and set "Autopay Minimum" for your credit cards.

### üìö Lesson 2.3: Utilization Magic (The 30/10/0 Rule)
*   **Objective:** How to hack your utilization date to maximize your score.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Utilization is 30% of your score. Most people fail this because of *timing*.
    *   **The Secret:** There are two dates.
        1.  **Due Date:** When you must pay to avoid fees.
        2.  **Statement Date (Reporting Date):** When the bank takes a "snapshot" of your balance and sends it to the bureaus.
    *   If you pay on the *Due Date*, the *Statement Date* has usually already passed. The high balance is already on your report!
    *   **The Hack:** Pay your balance down to 1-3% roughly **3 days BEFORE the Statement Date**.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "Sniper Payment"** ‚Äî Don't just pay. Pay at the exact right moment to show a $0 or low balance to the world.
*   **Moo Point:** Call your bank or check your bill. Find out your "Statement Closing Date" (NOT the Due Date).

### üìö Lesson 2.4: Age & Wisdom (Patience)
*   **Objective:** Why closing old cards hurts you and how to preserve history.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Lenders love stability. They want to see you've managed credit for a long time (15% of Score).
    *   **Average Age of Accounts (AAoA):** The math average of all your open accounts.
    *   **The Mistake:** Promoting "Closing old cards." When you close a 10-year-old card, you erase 10 years of history from this calculation. Your score drops.
    *   **The Fix:** Keep your oldest cards open forever. Even if the rewards suck.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "Anchor Card"** ‚Äî Your oldest card is the Anchor of your ship. Put a tiny subscription (like Netflix or iCloud, $1/mo) on it and set it to autopay. Throw the card in a drawer. It will build your history while you sleep.
*   **Moo Point:** Identify your oldest account. Ensure it's active and has a small transaction to keep it from being closed for inactivity.

### üìö Lesson 2.5: The Mix (Variety is Spice)
*   **Objective:** Understanding Revolving vs. Installment credit.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Credit Mix is 10% of your score. It shows you can handle different *types* of stress.
    *   **Revolving Credit:** Credit Cards. Balance goes up and down. Requires discipline.
    *   **Installment Credit:** Loans (Auto, Student, Personal). Fixed payment every month. Requires consistency.
    *   A profile with only credit cards is "thin." A profile with both is "thick" and robust.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "Healthy Diet"** ‚Äî Don't just eat cards. If you don't have a loan, consider a "Credit Builder Loan" (like Self or Credit Strong) to add that missing vitamin to your report.
*   **Moo Point:** Audit your report. do you have both types? If not, note it for your Sophomore strategy.

### üìö Lesson 2.6: The Inquiry Buffer
*   **Objective:** Hard vs. Soft Pulls and how to protect your score.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   Every time you ask for credit, it counts as a "Hard Inquiry" (New Credit - 10%).
    *   **Hard Inquiry:** You applied for a loan. Drops score 3-5 points. Stays for 2 years.
    *   **Soft Inquiry:** You checked your own score, or a lender pre-approved you. **Does NOT affect score.**
    *   Too many Hard Inquiries in a short time makes you look desperate and risky.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **The "Velvet Rope"** ‚Äî Treat your credit report like a VIP club. Don't let just any lender in. Only let them run your credit if you are 99% sure you will buy/get approved. Protect the gate.
*   **Moo Point:** Count your hard inquiries in the last 24 months. If you have more than 5, you need to "Garden" (stop applying) for 6 months.

### üìö Lesson 2.7: The Dispute Primer (Intro to War)
*   **Objective:** Introduction to challenging negative items.
*   **Core Teaching:**
    *   This is where we go on offense. If there is a negative item (Late payment, Collection) on your report, you have the right to challenge it.
    *   **The Mindset:** We don't ask nicely for forgiveness ("Goodwill"). We demand proof of accuracy ("Validation").
    *   **The Process:** You identify an error (wrong balance, wrong date, open when should be closed). You send a letter effectively saying: "Prove this is 100% accurate or remove it."
    *   This is the core of Credit Repair. It's not magic; it's bureaucracy combat.
*   **Credit U Framework:** **"Factual Disputing"** ‚Äî We don't lie. We find the data errors. "This account says open in May, but my records show June. Inaccurate. Delete."
*   **Moo Point:** Open the "Credit Lab" tool and look at a sample dispute letter. That is your weapon for Level 3.
